Founder

Allison Crank

Designer, researcher, and architect working at the intersection of spatial computing, AI, and luxury technology.

Trained in architecture at Wellesley College (magna cum laude) and MIT, with graduate work at Design Academy Eindhoven (cum laude) and EPFL. I bring spatial logic to digital systems ‚Äî understanding experience as choreography, not just interface.

Based in Paris, I've collaborated with National Geographic, Google, Meta, MIT, La Biennale di Venezia, and Dreamscape. I've designed for Audemars Piguet and helped lead IMPULSE ‚Äî an Emmy-nominated mixed-reality experience about perception and neurodiversity, shown at Venice, Cannes, Tribeca, and Annecy.

My work spans XR, AI-driven interfaces, spatial computing, and culture-care systems for hospitals and museums. I've led platforms including LazyBrain, Pix.T, and Polaris.

I teach at HSLU in Lucerne and at the Architectural Association Visiting School in Melbourne. My research has appeared in Leonardo (MIT Press), and I mentor at the Venice VR College.

I design for how people feel, move, and make meaning in complex, hybrid worlds.
